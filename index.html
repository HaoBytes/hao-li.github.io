About Me

Hao is a PhD from the Text Mining and NLP group at the University of Manchester, UK. His research centres on Natural Language Process, Computer Vision and Health AI, with a recent focus on foundation models and diffusion modeling, especially in the healthcare domain. His work has been published in leading venues such as ICML, KDD, ACL, EMNLP, and NAACL, accumulating over a hundred citations.

    Research Statement

    Time-Series Analysis

    My core research centers on time-series modeling, especially for irregular temporal data. I develop models capable of handling multi-frequency sequences, enabling improved forecasting, generation, and understanding across domains such as healthcare.

    I explore neural ODEs, frequency-aware Transformers to address temporal irregularity and heterogeneity, particularly in real-world data like EHR.

    In my recent work, I emphasize controllable time-series generation using diffusion-based models, combining textual prompts and structural constraints to create realistic, domain-specific synthetic sequences for simulation, augmentation, and stress testing.

    Argument Mining

    During the PhD, I conducted research in argument mining and natural language summarization, focusing on detecting, summarizing, and generating structured arguments from text. My work includes the creation of multi-task datasets for claim detection, evidence selection, and preference-based evaluation, as well as models that generate debate scripts via large language diffusion models.

    I am especially interested in how LLMs can model human argumentation with logical sufficiency, coherence, and persuasion. My research aims to advance both theoretical insights and practical tools for education, deliberation, and AI alignment with social reasoning.
