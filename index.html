About Me

Hao is a PhD from the Text Mining and NLP group at the University of Manchester, UK. His research centres on Natural Language Process, Computer Vision and Health AI, with a recent focus on foundation models and diffusion modeling, especially in the healthcare domain. His work has been published in leading venues such as ICML, KDD, ACL, EMNLP, and NAACL, accumulating over a hundred citations.

    Research Statement

    Foundation Models

    My core research focuses on foundation models that generalize across tasks, datasets, and modalities. I emphasize building scalable architectures with modular components, such as sparse experts, continuous-time position embeddings, and adaptive attention, that enable forecasting, generation, and decision-making from temporal, textual, and multimodal inputs. I am particularly interested in domain-adaptive pretraining strategies that bridge the gap between general-purpose modeling and application-specific requirements.

    Time-Series Analysis

    My research on time-series modeling, focus on learning from irregular temporal data. I am interested in developing models that can robustly handle multivariate dynamics, long-range dependencies, and missing values. By combining insights from signal processing, representation learning, and sequential modeling, I aim to improve forecasting and generation across real-world domains such as healthcare, finance, and system monitoring.

    AI for Healthcare

    I am committed to developing AI systems that can support clinical understanding, decision-making, and care planning. My research focuses on learning from real-world medical dataâ€”especially electronic health records (EHR), longitudinal time-series, and clinical narrativesâ€”while addressing challenges such as irregular sampling, label scarcity, and domain shift. I prioritize model interpretability and safety, aiming to design systems that can assist medical professionals in high-stakes, dynamic environments.

    Multimodal Large Language Models

    My research in multimodal large language models focuses on improving how language models interact with visual inputs, such as images and videos. I explore the design of architectures and adaptation strategies that allow LLMs to efficiently process and reason over visual content. This includes methods for token compression, cross-modal knowledge distillation, and vision-guided pruning. I am particularly interested in how to enhance model efficiency and robustness in vision-language tasks, such as egocentric action recognition, cross-modal retrieval, and instruction-following with grounded inputs.

    Argument Mining

    My research in argument mining centers on understanding how people express reasoning, claims, and evidence in natural language. I work on building systems that can identify argumentative components, extract key points, and generate structured summaries or persuasive texts. By combining large language models with retrieval, generation, and evaluation modules, I aim to support applications such as deliberation assistance, educational tools, and alignment with human reasoning.
